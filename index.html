Introduction

Hello, my name is John Patrick Hartley. I was born on June 10th, 2001, in sunny San Diego, California. I have a passion for gaming and love spending my free time watching YouTube and diving into strategy and fighting games. Whether I'm exploring complex tactics in strategy titles or sharpening my reflexes in intense fighting games, I enjoy the challenges and excitement they bring. My love for games has also inspired me to pursue a deeper understanding of game development and programming, turning my hobby into a pathway toward a creative and dynamic career.

Astreya - 2.5D retro game

Astreya is my most recent project, developed over the course of a month with a team of three other people. Our goal was to create a game from scratch using Vulkan and the Gateware API. This project was not only a blast to work on but also my first experience collaborating with a more structured team and using Jira for project management. Inspired by Asteroids, we set out to recreate the classic game by the second week of class before expanding on it with our own innovations. This experience taught me a lot about real teamwork, further honed my programming skills, and even gave me another opportunity to tackle audio implementation—this time with success. The hard work paid off, as our professor recognized Astreya as one of the best projects the class had ever seen. It will now be used as the example project for future students, which makes it even more rewarding.

Robocode - AI robot dueling

Robocode was a project from my Artificial Intelligence class, where the goal was to build a robot capable of defeating the school's own AI opponent. Since I genuinely loved both the class and AI as a subject, I dedicated a lot of time and effort to refining my robot's strategy. The competitive aspect made it even more exciting, as I was driven not just to complete the assignment but to showcase my work and test it against other students' creations. It was a rewarding experience that fueled my passion for AI and strategic problem-solving.

StoneHenge - Texturing and graphics

StoneHenge was, without a doubt, one of the most challenging assignments I've faced in my degree program. Given to me during my graphics class, it demanded a significant amount of dedication and effort to complete. More than any other class, this project deepened my understanding of lighting and textures, pushing me to refine my skills and problem-solving abilities. Despite the difficulty, it was an incredibly valuable learning experience—one that tested my limits and helped me grow as a developer.

Take A Chance - Unity FPS

Take A Chance was my first real attempt at making a game, developed alongside a classmate as part of a two-month-long midterm project. It was our introduction to Unity, as well as our first experience using Scrum and Trello to organize tasks and communicate progress effectively. The assignment came with specific requirements, such as implementing audio support, which pushed us to learn and apply new skills throughout development. More than just a school project, this experience was a significant milestone for me. It marked the moment when game development became more than just an interest—it became something tangible that I could build and bring to life. Looking back, Take A Chance remains one of my most memorable and rewarding experiences in school, and it will always hold a special place in my heart.

Education

Attending Full Sail University significantly enhanced my game development and programming skills through its hands-on, project-based curriculum. The university's focus on real-world applications allowed me to work with industry-standard tools and technologies, giving me practical experience in coding, game design, and software development. Collaborative projects with peers helped me develop problem-solving skills and taught me the importance of teamwork in a development environment. The experienced faculty, many of whom are industry professionals, provided valuable insights and guidance, helping me refine my technical abilities and creative approach. Overall, Full Sail University equipped me with the knowledge and confidence needed to excel in the competitive world of game development.
